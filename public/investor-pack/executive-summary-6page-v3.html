The Builder's Dilemma

Three critical failures of the traditional model:

01. Time to Market

6-12 months to MVP. By the time traditional dev teams deliver, market windows have closed. Competitors have moved. Customer needs have evolved. The pace of modern business demands iteration in days, not quarters.

02. Equity Dilution

Technical co-founders take 20-40%. Non-technical founders are forced to give away significant equity just to build their product. This creates misaligned incentives and reduces founder control at the most critical early stages.

03. Capital Inefficiency

90% fail before revenue. High burn rates on engineering talent consume runway before product-market fit is achieved. Founders run out of capital learning what customers actually want.
